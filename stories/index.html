Kelp Gull

These photos capture the raw, opportunistic feeding behavior of a juvenile New Zealand Kelp Gull. With its still-developing plumage, the young gull eagerly tears chunks of flesh from a fish carcass, demonstrating its vital role as a scavenger along the coastline. The act of tearing and manipulating the fish with its powerful beak showcases the bird's adaptability and resourcefulness in utilizing available food sources. This feeding frenzy, common among juvenile gulls, highlights their drive to consume as much sustenance as possible, contributing to their growth and survival within the dynamic coastal ecosystem.



Pied Shgags

New Zealand Pied Shags are known for their aggressive territorial behavior, particularly during the breeding season. These birds will vigorously defend their nests and nesting sites from rivals, engaging in a variety of displays and physical confrontations. Bill-clapping, wing-flapping, and posturing are common tactics used to intimidate opponents, and these displays can quickly escalate into full-blown fights involving pecking and biting. Fighting serves multiple purposes for Pied Shags, including establishing dominance hierarchies within colonies and securing access to valuable resources such as food and nesting sites. The intensity of these interactions highlights the importance of territoriality and resource defense in the lives of these fascinating seabirds.
